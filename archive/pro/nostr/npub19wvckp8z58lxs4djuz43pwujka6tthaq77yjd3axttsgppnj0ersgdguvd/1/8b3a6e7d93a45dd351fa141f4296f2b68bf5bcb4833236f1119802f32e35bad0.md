**AUTHOR**, **AUTHORITY**, and **AUTHORIZED** all come from **AUTHOR**.

I just realized that **AUTHOR**, **AUTHORITY**, and **AUTHORIZED** are actually related in spirit, even though in modern English they have taken on distinct meanings.

That connection suddenly clarified something foundational in Continuum’s model:

Only an **AUTHORized NPUB** can **AUTHOR**.

A public identity (**NPUB**) may be present locally in read-only form (**no NSEC**), but it cannot sign, or originate content unless it carries signing authority.

To authorize means, quite literally, to grant the right to perform a specific action.

In Continuum, that means the right to author specific content or perform specific actions on behalf of that identity.

This feels like more than wordplay.

It expresses the architecture itself:

**authorship requires authority.**
