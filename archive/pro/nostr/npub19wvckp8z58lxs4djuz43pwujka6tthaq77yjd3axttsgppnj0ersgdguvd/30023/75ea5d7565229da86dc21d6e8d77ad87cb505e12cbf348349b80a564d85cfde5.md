# Title
My first sats purchase of *Building Continuum*

## Summary

## Tags
#Bitcoin #Sats #BuildingContinuum #AuthorJourney #BuilderJournal #Sovereignty

## Content
Today I received my first purchase of *Building Continuum: Local Authority in a Networked World* in sats.

I have to be honest — that meant more to me than I expected.

Not because of the amount.

Because of what it represents.

The last year has been difficult in ways that are hard to separate.

Financial losses.

The emotional weight of 2025.

The losses in Schedule D.

The real estate losses.

The pressure from StartNation not yet really bearing fruit.

The thousands of hours in Continuum.

Writing and publishing the book.

Trying to keep building while often feeling like very little signal comes back.

So when something concrete happens, it lands differently.

This was the first sats purchase of the book.

And because of what the book is about, it felt deeply aligned.

*Building Continuum* is fundamentally about ownership.

Local authority.

Durable authorship.

Identity that does not begin on someone else’s server.

Writing that is not reduced to feed engagement.

A different posture toward digital life.

To receive support for that work in Bitcoin feels coherent.

The medium matches the message.

That is what struck me today.

This was not just “someone bought the book.”

Someone chose to support *this* book in sats.

There is something beautiful about that.

The book argues that we need better foundations.

Bitcoin represents that same instinct in another domain.

Ownership.

Directness.

Self-custody.

Responsibility.

So when that signal came back today, it felt less like a random transaction and more like confirmation that the ideas are beginning to resonate with at least some people.

I am not trying to overstate it.

It is one purchase.

I know that.

But builders live on early signals.

Especially after long periods of uncertainty.

A single signal can matter a lot.

It does not prove the future.

It does not guarantee momentum.

But it reminds you that the work is not disappearing into the void.

Someone saw value.

Someone acted on it.

Someone crossed the line from reading into support.

That encouraged me.

I needed that today.

Because sometimes the emotional weight of building can become heavy.

You start to wonder whether the work is landing anywhere.

You wonder whether the book mattered.

You wonder whether the software will ever reach enough people to justify the effort.

Then a small but deeply aligned signal arrives.

And it reminds you why you started.

Today was one of those days.
