Saturday, April 4, 2026

The day after.

Holy Saturday always carries the feeling of work continuing beneath the surface, even when nothing visible seems to be moving.

The morning began with fellowship at John Fong’s home, which was a grounding way to start the day.

Much of the rest of the day moved into the practical weight of finishing 2025 taxes. From roughly 8 PM until 2 AM, I stayed focused on pushing the return toward completion — sorting through capital losses, real estate losses, distributions, healthcare forms, and carryforwards.

It was not glamorous work, but it was necessary work.

In some ways, the day itself mirrored Holy Saturday: quiet labor, unseen progress, and the sense that resolution was close even if it had not yet fully broken through.
