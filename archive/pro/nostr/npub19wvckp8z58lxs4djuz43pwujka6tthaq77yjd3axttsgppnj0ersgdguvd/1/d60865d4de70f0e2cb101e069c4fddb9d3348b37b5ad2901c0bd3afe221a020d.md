**July 19, 2026**

I sometimes feel like I am building something orthogonal to the current culture of Nostr.

Much of Nostr is understandably focused on the stream: publishing, following, replying, reacting, zapping, and moving on to whatever appears next. Most clients are designed around that experience.

There is nothing wrong with a “gm,” a meme, a photograph, or a quick reaction. These things may seem ephemeral when they appear, but after months and years they can carry surprising weight. Repeated greetings become a record of presence. Likes become a record of attention. Photographs recover people, places, and periods we had almost forgotten.

An old photo from Bitcoin 2025 recently surfaced in Continuum’s Media Library. I had mostly forgotten it, but Derek Ross remembered it.

The photo did not just resurface. A relationship and a shared moment resurfaced with it.

That is the problem I keep thinking about.

What does the stream become after we have lived in it for five or ten years? How do we find the conversations that mattered, see how relationships developed, recover earlier versions of our writing, or rediscover something whose importance was not obvious when it was posted?

A relay can preserve an event. That is important. But preserving an event is not the same as preserving its place in a life.

Continuum uses Nostr, but its vision is larger than Nostr.

Nostr is an important part of the foundation because it gives us portable identities and signed events that are not owned by a single social platform. But our digital lives are spread across much more than one protocol: files, photographs, writing, conversations, projects, accounts, and relationships scattered among tools that will inevitably change.

Continuum is intended to become a durable home for that history—a place where the parts of your digital life can remain connected and understandable even as clients, services, protocols, and AI systems come and go.

It is not meant to replace every tool you use. It is meant to preserve continuity across them.

Nostr gives us a stream.

Continuum helps the stream become a history—and helps that history remain yours.

What have you posted that might mean more to you now than it did at the time?
