(English version)

That analogy is interesting, but I think it mixes two different things.

Privacy in wallets is a user choice to protect themselves within the system.
Centralized control is an imposition on the system as a whole.

In the first case, you decide.
In the second, someone decides for you.

Bitcoin works precisely because it does not depend on central control to exist—and privacy is an optional layer, not a justification for more control.

Risk exists in any system.
The question is: who bears that risk—the individual or the authority?
