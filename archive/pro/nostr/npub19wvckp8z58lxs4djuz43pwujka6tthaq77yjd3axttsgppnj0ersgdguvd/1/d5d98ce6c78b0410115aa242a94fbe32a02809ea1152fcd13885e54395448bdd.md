I wrote this about two months after starting Continuum. 
(about 1 year ago) 

Reading it today, it's interesting to see what has changed—and what hasn't.

At the time I thought Continuum was primarily a sovereign publishing and identity framework.

Today I think of it as something broader: a local-first home for our digital lives.

Some ideas were there from the beginning. Others—relationship memory, media libraries, version history, and local AI—didn't emerge until much later.

I wouldn't rewrite this article. I think it's valuable as a snapshot of how the project was evolving.
