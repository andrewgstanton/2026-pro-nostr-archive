Summary:

Sabbath places markers in time.

Without markers, time becomes a blur.

Weeks merge together.  
Months disappear.  
Years pass faster than expected.

But Sabbath interrupts that blur.

It reminds us that not every day is meant to be the same.

One day is set apart.

One day is different.

Sabbath gives structure to time so life does not dissolve into an endless sequence of tasks.

> “God blessed the seventh day and made it holy.”  
> — Genesis 2:3
