## Tuesday, April 7, 2026 — Native App Stability + Distribution Clarity (v1.6.3 → v1.6.5)

Today was a major stabilization day for the native application flow.

I moved through **v1.6.3, v1.6.4, and v1.6.5** with a strong focus on the launch experience and distribution clarity.

The biggest technical issue addressed was the **launch weirdness on native builds**.

This was one of those subtle UX issues that can quietly undermine confidence in the product.

The fix now ensures that:

- if Continuum is already running
- a double-click always launches correctly
- any stale or lingering Continuum process is terminated first
- the app restarts cleanly and predictably

That behavior now feels much more native.

This matters more than it may seem.

For most users, the app either just opens or it feels broken.

Reducing that ambiguity is a major quality-of-life improvement.

On the packaging side, **v1.6.4 and v1.6.5 introduced DMG build support**, which is another major milestone for macOS distribution.

This brings the experience closer to what users expect from a polished desktop application.

Just as importantly, I also updated the **downloads page and pricing page** to more clearly explain the current open-source posture.

I wanted to remove any mixed messaging around:

- what is currently open source
- what is not yet open source
- what patrons are supporting
- how this aligns with the long-term vision

That clarity matters.

Continuum is built around sovereignty and first principles, so the messaging around openness, hosted value, and patron support needed to be explicit.

Overall, these last two days were less about new features and more about making Continuum feel increasingly real as a product people can install, trust, and use daily.

That feels like real progress.
