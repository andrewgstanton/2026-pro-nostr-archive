## At the Conference

Stayed at the conference until around 5:30 PM.

A few things stood out pretty quickly.

First — the schedule felt… looser this year.

Multiple speakers were late. Not just a few minutes — sometimes 15 minutes, even 30. It made the whole thing feel a bit less tight compared to last year, which I remember being more dialed in.

Second — small detail, but noticeable — the T-shirts this year felt kind of boring.

Not a big deal, and I’ll probably still get one. But compared to last year, they didn’t stand out.

---

## Michael Saylor & STRC

:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} spent a good amount of time talking about a new preferred stock — STRC (Digital Credit).

The idea (as he explained it):
- price is intended to stay around $100  
- monthly dividend  
- ~11% annualized yield  

He went deep into how he engineered it.

From a technical and financial standpoint, it was interesting.

But I have mixed feelings.

On one hand, it’s impressive — structuring something like that, aligning it with a Bitcoin treasury strategy, creating yield.

On the other hand… it starts to feel like financial engineering again.

Not necessarily wrong. But it’s a different direction than the simplicity that drew a lot of us to Bitcoin in the first place.

Still processing that one.

## Bitcoin Magazine Presence

One thing that stood out more this year:

:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1} had a very visible presence — especially around books.

They were actively promoting titles from their own authors.

Which makes sense — they’re part of :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2} and also run the conference.

But it did add to that feeling I mentioned earlier:

A bit more structured.  
A bit more commercial.

Not necessarily a bad thing.

But it changes the tone compared to something that feels more purely grassroots.

## General Feeling

The event definitely has energy.

No question.

But it feels different than last year.

Not worse exactly.

Just… different.

Hard to fully put into words yet, but you can feel it:
- slightly less sharp  
- slightly more structured  
- slightly more commercial in tone  

I need another day or two to really understand what that shift is.
